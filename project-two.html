PROJECT OVERVIEW

Safewheel is a fictional non-profit organization that's committed to keeping youth safe and educated about drinking and driving.

Safewheel wants to build a website that could help the organization achieve its business goals: raising awareness about impaired driving and increasing the Donation conversion rate.

üë§ user ANALYSIS

Based on the client's business goals and organization's information, we started developing Archetypes (aka ideal users' profiles). It is essential for our development team to understand who the ideal users of the website are. We did articulate 2 groups of ideal users. The 1st group is our primary users who are concerned parents, charitable seniors, former alcoholics, or family members of alcoholics. The 2nd group is considered as our secondary users who are youth& teenagers.


Next steps, we created a persona. In UX design, the persona is used to articulate the ideal user. For us, the UX strategists, we wanted to get to know these users, empathize with their frustrations, and understand what they want out of their experience on this website. We used xtensio.com/user-persona-template for our persona development. 3 different personas were created clearly representing 3 types of our ideal users as per defined.


This action will help our development team focus on a design and integrate functionality that would be appropriate.

üîé BASELINE REPORTS

To help our team understand how well the existing site has done in the past and will be a good way to measure how our new website performing (in the future) against the old site (hence 'baseline'), we extracted the data from provided materials (by instructors) and took some baseline statistics review. We acknowledged some conversion data like sales, or downloads, or volunteers that occurred in the previous year and had an overview of the current site's performance such as time-on-site, page views, users, and acquisitions.

The research we did here will help our client and our developer understands how effective the new design is after we launch the new site later on.

üìÅ COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

A competitive analysis helped us explore the possibilities with our client's website. By using 3rd party tool such as Nibbler and Hubspot Website Grader, 3 different competitor sites (MADD, ALCOHOL CHANGE and AUADD) were reviewed to give us insight into what to build into the new site.

Some findings were highlighted such as: user tends to satisfy with informative homepage having all information on one-page, simple navigation with labelling system is easy to understand, having great design aesthetics with interesting functionality are these key winning points to keep user engaged with our site.

üí° IDEATION

At first, we spent some time brainstorming about the organization. Not just the website, but everything about the industry, which is related to impaired driving. By using tobloef.com/text2mindmap tool, A concept map is created to help us find new content, gave us insight into labeling and navigation. Moreover, it gave our development team perspective on what the industry is about. Some main labels are firmed like: Distracted Driving, Driving Change, Drugged Driving, Drinking and Driving, besides Donate page. Then, we used app.diagrams.net to develop a sitemap as a blueprint of the new website. One detailed user flow is also created to demo a Donation process which has some specific requirements we felt were somewhat complex to code.
